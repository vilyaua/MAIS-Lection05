# Ukrainian P1-SUN Drone Interceptor

## Introduction / Overview
The P1-SUN is a Ukrainian interceptor drone developed by SkyFall, a defense technology company known for modern unmanned systems. Introduced publicly at the Dubai Airshow in late 2025, it is designed specifically for intercepting hostile drones and some types of cruise missiles. Unlike conventional strike or surveillance UAVs, the P1-SUN focuses on rapid aerial target interception.

## Design and Key Features
- **Modular Architecture:** The drone features a modular 3D-printed airframe allowing rapid swapping of components like camera, radio, battery, and payload for mission adaptability.
- **High Speed and Maneuverability:** It achieves speeds up to 450 km/h (enhanced from the initial 300 km/h) enabling pursuit of fast-moving targets.
- **Operational Altitude:** Capable of operating up to 5,000 meters in altitude, suitable for low- to mid-altitude interceptions.
- **Control:** Operates under FPV (First Person View) semi-autonomous control, providing the operator real-time visual feed for precise engagements.
- **Production Scale:** Designed for mass production with thousands produced monthly, leveraging 3D printing to reduce costs and manufacturing time.
- **Design Input:** Developed with feedback from Ukraine’s frontline personnel, focusing on combat realities such as intercepting Russian loitering munitions (e.g., Geran-2/Shahed) and even hostile helicopters.

## Operational Use
- Primarily intended as a short-range counter to strike UAVs threatening cities, military, and critical infrastructure.
- Deployable in layered air defense networks to create low-altitude defensive barriers.
- Effective against slower cruise missiles and possibly low-flying helicopters.
- Vulnerable to electronic warfare jamming due to reliance on communication links.
